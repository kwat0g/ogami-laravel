  Full Recruitment Workflow (7 Phases)

  Requisition вҶ’ Job Posting вҶ’ Application вҶ’ Interview(s) вҶ’ Offer вҶ’
  Pre-Employment вҶ’ Hiring

  Phase 1 вҖ” Requisition

  Department head creates a hiring request (draft). Submits for approval
  (pending_approval). HR/manager approves or rejects (SoD enforced вҖ” requester вү 
   approver). Once approved, opens it to accept applications (open). Can be put
  on_hold and resumed. Auto-closes when headcount is filled.

  Phase 2 вҖ” Job Posting

  HR creates a posting from the approved requisition (draft вҶ’ published). Tracks
   views, internal/external visibility, close date. Can expire or be manually
  closed, then reopened.

  Phase 3 вҖ” Application

  Candidates apply against a posting. HR reviews (new вҶ’ under_review вҶ’
  shortlisted). Terminal states: hired, rejected, withdrawn. One application per
   candidate per posting enforced.

  Phase 4 вҖ” Interview (Multi-Round)

  Multiple rounds per application. Types: HR screening, technical, panel,
  one-on-one, final. Each round: scheduled вҶ’ in_progress вҶ’
  completed/cancelled/no_show. Evaluator submits a scorecard with criteria
  scores + overall score + recommendation (endorse/reject/hold).

  Phase 5 вҖ” Offer

  HR prepares a draft offer (salary, position, employment type, expiry). Sends
  to candidate. Auto-expires after expires_at via ExpireOffersJob. Candidate
  accepts/rejects. One offer per application.

  Phase 6 вҖ” Pre-Employment Checklist

  After offer acceptance, HR initializes a checklist (manually today). 11
  document types (NBI, medical, TIN, SSS, etc.). Each requirement: pending вҶ’ 
  submitted вҶ’ verified/rejected/waived. Must be fully completed or waived before
   hiring.

  Phase 7 вҖ” Hiring (Employee Creation)

  HiringService.hire() creates the Employee record from the candidate's data,
  assigns EMP-YYYY-###### code, sets is_active = false and onboarding_status = 
  documents_pending. Links to the HR onboarding module.

в—Ҹ Here is the full plan before we proceed:

  ---
  What Will Be Built

  1. HR ManagerвҖ“Only Application Guards

  Two new permissions added (recruitment.applications.create,
  recruitment.applications.delete) assigned exclusively to the manager role.
  ApplicationPolicy gets create() and delete() methods вҖ” delete only allowed
  when application is still new or under_review.

  2. Department/Office Interview Assignment

  The interview_schedules table gets an interviewer_department_id column and
  interviewer_id becomes nullable. When scheduling an interview, the HR Manager
  can either:
  - Assign a specific person (e.g. an HR Head employee), or
  - Assign a department/office (e.g. Engineering Office вҖ” anyone in that dept
  can conduct it)

  A DB CHECK ensures at least one of the two must be set.

  3. VP Hiring Approval Gate (New Flow)

  Old flow: Pre-employment complete вҶ’ hire() вҶ’ Employee created immediately

  New flow:
  HR Manager fills full employee data form
      вҶ“ submitForApproval()
  Hiring {status: pending_vp_approval} + employee_data stored as JSONB
      вҶ“ VP notified
  VP approves вҶ’ Employee auto-created from stored data
  VP rejects  вҶ’ HR Manager notified, no Employee created

  HiringService.hire() splits into 3 methods: submitForApproval(), vpApprove(),
  vpReject(). New permission recruitment.hiring.approve given to vice_president
  only.

  4. Full Employee Data at Hire Time

  HireRequest expands from 4 fields to all Employee fields:
  - Identity: middle name, suffix, citizenship
  - Address: present + permanent
  - Payroll: pay basis, salary grade, supervisor, regularization date
  - Bank: bank name, account number
  - Government IDs: SSS, TIN, PhilHealth, Pag-IBIG (nullable, encrypted on
  creation)

  Employee is auto-created on VP approval using this stored data.

  ---
  My Suggestion on One Thing

  в–Һ "full employee record data must be added before fully hired"

  Rather than making ALL fields required on the hire form (which could block
  hiring if, say, the person hasn't opened a bank account yet), I'd suggest:
  - Required at hire time: present address, gender, DOB, pay basis, personal
  email
  - Nullable/optional at hire time: bank details, government IDs, permanent
  address, regularization date

  These optional fields can be completed during HR onboarding (the employee
  already has onboarding_status: documents_pending). This mirrors how Philippine
   companies actually work вҖ” you hire the person first, then collect the IDs
  over the first week.

  If you want ALL fields strictly required (hard block), I'll make them all
  required in HireRequest. Just say the word.
