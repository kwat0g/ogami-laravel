# Organizational Structure and Responsibilities

Below are the key roles in the organization along with their primary responsibilities. The structure follows a hierarchical model from executive management down to department heads.

---

## Executive Management

| Position | Responsibilities |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Chairman** | Oversees and supervises the overall status, direction, and performance of the company. |
| **President** | Maintains strong relationships with customers and company owners; ensures business growth and client satisfaction. |
| **Vice President** | Secures new projects and business opportunities from clients; approves major operational and financial requests. |

---

## Senior Management (Managers)

| Position | Responsibilities |
|----------|-----------------|
| **HR Manager** | Manages all human resource functions including recruitment, employee relations, and administrative operations. |
| **Plant Manager** | Oversees all plant operations, ensuring efficiency, safety, and productivity. |
| **Production Manager** | Supervises and coordinates all production activities to meet output targets. |
| **QC/QA Manager** | Manages quality control and quality assurance processes to ensure product standards are met. |
| **Mold Manager** | Oversees all operations related to mold design, maintenance, and usage. |

---

## Officers

| Position | Responsibilities |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Accounting Officer** | Handles financial management, including budgeting, payments, and financial reporting. |
| **General Administration Officer** | Supports human resources and general administrative functions of the company. |
| **Purchasing Officer** | Manages procurement activities, including ordering materials and coordinating with suppliers. |
| **ImpEx Officer** | Handles import and export operations, shipment documentation, and delivery coordination. |

---

## Department Heads

| Position | Responsibilities |
|----------|-----------------|
| **Warehouse Head** | Manages warehouse operations, including receiving, storage, and distribution of materials and finished goods. |
| **PPC Head (Production Planning and Control)** | Develops production plans, schedules deliveries (local and export), manages inventory, and ensures material availability. |
| **Maintenance Head** | Oversees maintenance of machines and equipment to ensure operational efficiency. |
| **Production Head** | Ensures production of goods meets customer requirements and production targets. |
| **Processing Head** | Conducts inspection and verification of all produced items before final approval. |
| **QC/QA Head** | Ensures quality standards are maintained for all products prior to delivery. |
| **Management System Head** | Oversees compliance with management systems such as ISO and IATF standards. |

---

## Organizational Hierarchy (Command Flow)

```
Chairman
   ↓
President
   ↓
Vice President
   ↓
Managers
   ↓
Department Heads
   ↓
Staff
```

- **Managers** provide strategic direction to Department Heads  
- **Department Heads** supervise daily operations and assign tasks  
- **Staff** execute operational tasks  

---

## Functional Insight for System Design

This structure is critical for ERP implementation because it defines:

- **User roles and permissions**
- **Approval hierarchy (workflow routing)**
- **Data ownership per department**
- **Access control levels**

Example:
- Only **Vice President** → final approval  
- **Manager** → validation level  
- **Head** → operational supervision  
- **Staff** → request initiation
